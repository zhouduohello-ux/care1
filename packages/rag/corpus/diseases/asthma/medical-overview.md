# Asthma Medical Overview

Asthma is a chronic inflammatory disease of the airways. It causes variable symptoms such as wheeze, cough, chest tightness, and shortness of breath. Symptom control can change from day to day and week to week.

Key markers of poor control include:
- Nighttime waking due to cough or wheeze.
- Need for a reliever inhaler more than 2 days per week.
- Activity limitation due to asthma.
- Frequent exacerbations or oral-steroid courses.

Severe symptoms (unable to speak, blue lips, no relief from reliever, peak flow <50% best) require emergency care — call 999 in the UK.
